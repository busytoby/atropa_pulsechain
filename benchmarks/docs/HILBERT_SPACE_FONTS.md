# Hilbert Space Fonts: A Non-Graph Approach

This document defines the **Hilbert Space Font Architecture**, a radical departure from traditional vector (graph-based) or raster (pixel-based) font systems.

In this paradigm, a glyph is not a sequence of lines or curves. It is a **State Vector** $|\Psiangle$ residing in the Hilbert Space $L^2(\mathbb{R}^2)$ of square-integrable functions over the 2D plane.

## The Concept

### 1. The Glyph as a Field
Instead of defining the *boundary* (contour), we define the **Density Function** (or Potential Field) $\psi(x, y)$.
*   Inside the glyph: $\psi(x, y) \approx 1.0$
*   Outside the glyph: $\psi(x, y) \approx 0.0$

This function is constructed by the **Superposition of 2D Standing Waves**.

### 2. The Basis (The "Alphabet" of Space)
We choose an orthonormal basis for our Hilbert Space, specifically the **2D Fourier Basis** (Double Fourier Series) on the interval $[-\pi, \pi]^2$:
$$ \phi_{nm}(x, y) = e^{i(nx + my)} $$

Any glyph $|\Psiangle$ can be represented as a matrix of complex coefficients $C_{nm}$:
$$ \psi(x, y) = \sum_{n=-N}^{N} \sum_{m=-M}^{M} C_{nm} \cdot e^{inx} \cdot e^{imy} $$

### 3. "Non-Graph" Rendering
Rendering is no longer "drawing lines". It is **Observation** (Measurement) of the field intensity.
To determine if a pixel at $(x, y)$ is lit:
1.  Project the position operator onto the state vector: $I = \langle x,y | \Psi angle$.
2.  Compute Intensity: $|\psi(x, y)|^2$.
3.  Threshold: If Intensity > $T$, emit pixel.

## RDNA4 & WMMA Implementation

This mathematical abstraction maps directly to **Matrix Multiplication**, making it ideal for the RDNA4 Tensor Cores (WMMA).

### Reconstruction as Matrix Math
To render a Tile of pixels (e.g., $16 	imes 16$ grid):
Let $X$ be a matrix of horizontal basis row vectors ($e^{inx}$).
Let $Y$ be a matrix of vertical basis column vectors ($e^{imy}$).
Let $C$ be the Glyph Coefficient Matrix.

The image tile $I$ is generated by:
$$ I = Y 	imes C 	imes X^T $$

*   **Step 1:** $T_{emp} = Y 	imes C$ (Vertical Wave Synthesis).
*   **Step 2:** $I = T_{emp} 	imes X^T$ (Horizontal Wave Synthesis).

This replaces complex ray-curve intersection logic with **two pure WMMA operations**.

## Advantages of the Hilbert Approach

1.  **Algebraic Evolution:**
    *   **Morphing:** $|\Psi_{new}angle = (1-t)|\Psi_Aangle + t|\Psi_Bangle$. Just linear interpolation of coefficients. No topology issues.
    *   **Filtering:** Low-pass filter (remove high $n,m$ coeffs) = Blur/Round corners.
    *   **Edgy:** Boost high frequency coeffs.

2.  **Holographic Properties:**
    *   The glyph information is distributed across the coefficients.
    *   You can "stream" resolution: Load low frequencies first for a fuzzy version, then add high frequencies to sharpen.

3.  **OCR / Identification:**
    *   Identification is simply the **Inner Product** (Dot Product) between the observed state and the reference state.
    *   Similarity $S = |\langle \Psi_{observed} | \Psi_{reference} angle|$.
    *   This is extremely fast compared to image correlation.
