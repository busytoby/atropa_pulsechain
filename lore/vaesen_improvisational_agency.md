## 1. The Anatomy of Desire in Control Theory

In cybernetics, "desire" is modeled as a **target setpoint (reference signal $r(t)$)** that the system actively attempts to reach by adjusting its control inputs.
* **Static Control (Passive)**: The target setpoint $r$ is a fixed constant. The system simply minimizes error:
  $$e(t) = r - y(t)$$
* **Agentic/Improvisational Control (Active)**: The setpoint $r(t)$ is not fixed. The agent continuously rewrites its own targets based on its internal emotional states ($\mathcal{E}(t)$) and historical environmental feedback ($y(t-1)$):
