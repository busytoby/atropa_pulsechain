# Lucas the Spider: Scurrying Plush Tutorial

This comprehensive tutorial will guide you step-by-step through making your very own fuzzy jumping spider plush inspired by **Lucas the Spider**, complete with a mechanical **scurrying action**!

## 🛠 Materials & Tools

### Fabrics
*   **Main Body (Fuzzy Brown):** 1/2 yard of high-pile faux fur, plush minky, or thick fleece.
*   **Accent Color (Tan/Cream):** 1/4 yard of matching low-pile minky or fleece (for the face markings, leg stripes, and belly).

### Hardware & Scurrying Mechanism
*   **The Scurrying Engine:** A **vibrating pull-string toy mechanism** (often sold as replacement parts for vibrating cat toys or crawling baby plushies). 
    *   *How it works:* When you pull the cord, the internal spring motor spins a tiny off-balance weight, causing the spider to crawl and jitter quickly across hard surfaces in short, energetic bursts.
*   **The Eyes:** Two large 30mm black plastic dome safety eyes.
*   **The Leg Skeleton:** 12-gauge or 14-gauge aluminum craft wire (approx. 6 feet) to make the legs bendable.
*   **Stuffing:** High-quality polyester fiberfill.

### Tools
*   Sewing machine or hand-sewing needle & heavy-duty thread.
*   Fabric scissors & pins.
*   Needle-nose pliers & wire cutters (to shape the leg wire).
*   Hot glue gun (optional).

---

## 📐 Pattern Piece Dimensions
Scale these shapes to your desired size (our target size is roughly **8 inches** wide when sitting).

1.  **Head:** Cut 2 matching circles (approx. 5" diameter) from the main brown fabric.
2.  **Body (Abdomen):** Cut 2 matching teardrop/oval shapes (approx. 7" long, 5" wide) from the main brown fabric.
3.  **Belly Plate:** Cut 1 oval (approx. 5" long, 3" wide) from the accent tan fabric. *Make sure to mark a small center point for the pull-cord exit.*
4.  **Legs (8 total):** Cut 16 tapered rectangles (approx. 5" long, tapering from 2" wide to 1" wide) from the main brown fabric.
5.  **Pedipalps (2 front feelers):** Cut 4 small oval tags (approx. 1.5" long, 0.75" wide) from the accent tan fabric.

## 🧵 Step-by-Step Construction

### Step 1: Crafting the Leg Armature
1.  **Cut the Wires:** Cut 4 pieces of aluminum wire, each about 14 inches long.
2.  **Create the Core:** Align the 4 wires side-by-side. Wrap the middle 2 inches of the bundle tightly with electrical tape. This taped center block is the "spine" that sits in the spider's head/body.
3.  **Safety Loops:** Use your pliers to bend the tip of each leg prong into a tiny loop. *This prevents the sharp wire ends from piercing through the fabric of the feet.*

---

### Step 2: Preparing the Scurry Motor & Belly
1.  **Locate the Motor Placement:** The vibrating box needs to sit in the very bottom center of the abdomen (on the belly plate) so that the vibration transfers directly to the floor.
2.  **Cord Exit Hole:** Cut a tiny hole in the center of the tan Belly Plate. Apply a fray check or a small amount of fabric glue around the edges of the hole so it doesn't unravel.
3.  **Thread the Pull-Ring:** Push the pull-string of the mechanism through the hole from the inside to the outside, leaving the plastic ring hanging underneath the spider's belly.
4.  **Secure the Motor:** Sew or glue a small pocket of scrap fabric onto the inside of the Belly Plate to hold the vibration box securely in place, keeping it from shifting around inside the stuffing.

---

### Step 3: Sewing the Face & Head
1.  **Attach the Eyes:** Mark two spots on the front Head piece, spaced closely together near the bottom center. Cut tiny slits, push the safety eyes through, and secure them tightly with the washers. 
2.  **Make the Pedipalps:** Pin the Pedipalp pieces in pairs (right sides facing). Sew around the curved edges, leaving the straight flat base open. Flip them right-side out, stuff them lightly, and stitch them directly below the eyes.

---

### Step 4: Connecting Legs to the Body
1.  **Pin to Body:** Lay the top Abdomen piece flat, right-side up. Pin the open bases of 4 legs along the left edge, and 4 legs along the right edge. The legs should be pointing inwards across the body.
2.  **Belly Plate sandwich:** Place the Abdomen belly piece (with the motor already mounted inside it) on top, right sides together.
3.  **Sew:** Sew around the perimeter of the body, securing the legs in the seam. Leave a 2-inch opening at the front (where the head will attach).
4.  **Turn:** Turn the body right-side out through the front opening. The legs and pull-string should now extend outward.

---

### Step 5: Inserting the Skeleton & Stuffing
1.  **Slide in the Wire:** Gently insert the wire armature through the front opening of the body, positioning it *above* the vibration box. Guide the wire prongs into the 8 leg sleeves.
2.  **Stuff the Legs:** Using small pinches of poly-fil, push stuffing into the legs around the wire. 
3.  **Stuff the Body:** Pack the main body with stuffing, placing the stuffing *around and on top of* the vibration box. Make sure the bottom of the vibration box rests flat against the belly fabric with minimal stuffing blocking it from the floor.

---

### Step 6: Attaching the Head
1.  **Sew the Head:** Sew the two Head circles right-sides together, leaving a 2-inch opening at the bottom. Turn right-side out and stuff the head firmly.
2.  **Connect Head to Body:** Slide the front of the wire spine slightly into the back of the head. Align the head onto the front of the body.
3.  **Final Stitching:** Hand-sew the head securely to the body using a strong ladder stitch, going around the neck joint twice.

---

## 🏁 Testing the Scurry!

Put Lucas on a flat, smooth floor (wood or tile works best). Hold his body, pull the string ring underneath him all the way out, and let him go! The high-frequency vibration will transfer through his belly and bendable wire legs, making him dart, buzz, and scurry across the room just like a real jumping spider.
